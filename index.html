The approach

Therapy that goes
beneath the surface

Most of my clients have already done some version of "the work." They've read the books. They understand their patterns intellectually. And yet something remains stuck — some part that braces, or shuts down, or keeps them cycling through the same dynamics no matter how much insight they have.

That's where Internal Family Systems comes in. IFS doesn't ask you to manage your difficult parts or reason your way out of them. It works by creating a genuine relationship with them — one where even the most protective, reactive, or ashamed parts of you can finally be understood rather than overridden.

The result isn't a quieter version of your problems. It's something more like freedom.

About my approach
Internal Family Systems (IFS)
The foundation of my work. IFS is evidence-based, non-pathologizing, and among the most effective approaches available for trauma, anxiety, depression, and relational patterns. I'm a Level 3 trained, Certified IFS Therapist.
EMDR
Integrated when appropriate for processing specific traumatic memories. EMDR and IFS work well together — EMDR helps reprocess stuck material while IFS provides the internal safety to do so.
Neuroaffirming care
I work with neurodivergent clients — particularly those with ADHD, autism, or high sensitivity — and have developed particular fluency with the ways standard approaches can miss the mark for people whose minds work differently. Neurology is not the problem we're solving.
Somatic awareness
The body holds what the mind can't fully articulate. Our work includes attention to what you notice physically — where you feel tension, contraction, or ease — as part of the healing process.
